Lesson 15 – Invitations & Going Out

Ask Arabs to meet up, go out, come to your house – and accept or refuse politely without sounding rude.

Level: A2 → early B1 Focus: Social Arabic Topic: Inviting / accepting / refusing Time: ~90 minutes

1. Overview – Why This Lesson Matters

This is how you stop being “the quiet desi guy” and start being part of the Arab group.

By the end of this lesson, you can:
  • Invite someone out: “Come to the house”, “Let’s go for tea”, “Come to the masjid”.
  • Accept invitations warmly: “with pleasure”, “of course”.
  • Refuse politely: “I’d love to, but…”, “Maybe another time, in shā’ Allah”.
  • Arrange a time and place with simple, clear questions.

2. Key Verbs & Phrases for Inviting

These 6 verbs cover 80% of your invitations.

ArabicTranslitMeaning
تِجِي؟tijī?Will you come?
تِجُوا؟tijū?Will you guys come?
تِرُوح؟tirūḥ?Will you go?
خَلّ نَرُوحkhall n-rūḥLet’s go
تَعَال / تَعَالِيtaʿāl / taʿālīcome (m/f)
مَرّ عَلَيْنَاmurr ʿalēnādrop by / pass by us

Mini examples

  • تِجِي البُكْرَه المَسْجِد مَعَانَا؟ – tijī bukrah il-masjid maʿānā? – Will you come to the masjid with us tomorrow?
  • خَلّ نَرُوح نَشْرَب شَاي – khall narūḥ nishrab shāy – Let’s go drink tea.
  • تَعَال عِنْدِنَا اليَوْم المَسَاء – taʿāl ʿindnā il-yōm il-masā – Come to our house this evening.

3. Accepting Invitations – “Yeah, I’m Down”

Arabs like warm, enthusiastic acceptance – not cold “okay”.

ArabicTranslitMeaning / Feel
إي والله ē wallah yeah, definitely / for sure
أُكِيد akīd of course
بِكُل سُرُور b-kull surūr with pleasure (polite)
يَاللَّه نَرُوح yallāh narūḥ come on, let’s go
إن شاء الله جَاي in shā’ Allāh jāy in shā’ Allah I’m coming
Mini Drill – Say It with Energy
Say each one out loud 5 times, with friendly tone:
  • إي والله
  • أُكِيد
  • يَاللَّه نَرُوح

4. Refusing Politely – Without Killing the Vibe

This is where Arabs really notice if your social Arabic is good.

ArabicTranslitMeaning / Usage
وَالله وِدِّي، بَسّ... wallah widdī, bass… Honestly I’d like to, but…
اليَوْم مَا أَقْدَر il-yōm mā agdar I can’t today
مُمْكِن مَرّة ثَانِيَة؟ mumkin marrah thāniyah? Maybe another time?
عِنْدِي شُغْل / مَوْعِد ʿindī shughol / mawʿid I have work / an appointment
إن شاء الله المَرّة الجَايَة in shā’ Allāh il-marrah il-jāyah In shā’ Allah next time

Examples

  • وَالله وِدِّي، بَسّ اليَوْم مَا أَقْدَر، عِنْدِي شُغْل – wallah widdī, bass il-yōm mā agdar, ʿindī shughol – Honestly I’d like to, but today I can’t, I have work.
  • مُمْكِن مَرّة ثَانِيَة؟ إن شاء الله المَرّة الجَايَة – mumkin marrah thāniyah? in shā’ Allāh il-marrah il-jāyah – Maybe another time? In shā’ Allah next time.

6. Dialogue 1 – Simple Invite After Masjid

This is like inviting your Yemeni / Saudi / Syrian mate after ʿIshā’.

Arabic
أَنْت: تِجِي مَعَانَا المَقْهَى بَعْد العِشَا؟
صَاحِبَك: إي والله، يَلّا نَرُوح.
أَنْت: نِشْرَب شَاي، نِتْكَلَّم شُوَيَّة.
صَاحِبَك: طَيِّب، أَنَا الحِين أَرُوح البَيْت، بَعْدِين أَمُرّ عَلَيْكُمْ.
Transliteration
anta: tijī maʿānā il-maqhā baʿd il-ʿishāʼ?
ṣāḥibak: ē wallah, yallā narūḥ.
anta: nishrab shāy, nitkallam shwayyah.
ṣāḥibak: ṭayyib, anā il-ḥīn arūḥ il-bayt, baʿdēn amurr ʿalēkum.
English
You: Will you come with us to the café after ʿIshā’?
Your friend: Yeah, for sure – let’s go.
You: We’ll drink tea and chat a bit.
Your friend: Okay, I’ll go home now, then I’ll pass by you guys.
Personalise
Change “café” to “gym” / “park” / “restaurant” and rewrite.

7. Dialogue 2 – Saying No Politely

Imagine you really can’t go, but you don’t want to sound cold.

Arabic
صَاحِبَك: تِجِي بُكْرَه المَسَاء نَرُوح مَطْعَم يَمَنِي؟
أَنْت: وَالله وِدِّي، بَسّ بُكْرَه مَا أَقْدَر، عِنْدِي مَوْعِد.
صَاحِبَك: طَيِّب، مُمْكِن الجُمُعَة؟
أَنْت: إن شاء الله الجُمُعَة أَقْدَر، نِتْفَق بُكْرَه علَى الوَقْت.
صَاحِبَك: خَلَاص، مَا فِي مُشْكِلَة.
Transliteration
ṣāḥibak: tijī bukrah il-masā narūḥ maṭʿam yamani?
anta: wallah widdī, bass bukrah mā agdar, ʿindī mawʿid.
ṣāḥibak: ṭayyib, mumkin al-jumʿah?
anta: in shāʼ Allāh al-jumʿah agdar, nitfiq bukrah ʿala al-waqt.
ṣāḥibak: khalāṣ, mā fī mushkilah.
English
Your friend: Will you come tomorrow evening so we can go to a Yemeni restaurant?
You: Honestly I’d like to, but tomorrow I can’t, I have an appointment.
Your friend: Okay, maybe Friday?
You: In shā’ Allah Friday I can, we’ll agree on the time tomorrow.
Your friend: Done, no problem.

10. End-of-Lesson Quiz

A. Translate to Arabic
  1. Will you come with us to the restaurant tonight?
  2. Let’s go drink tea after the masjid.
  3. Honestly, I’d like to, but today I can’t.
  4. Maybe another time, in shā’ Allah.
  5. When? What time? Where will we meet?
Sample answers
  • تِجِي مَعَانَا المَطْعَم اليَوْم المَسَاء؟
  • خَلّ نَرُوح نِشْرَب شَاي بَعْد المَسْجِد.
  • وَالله وِدِّي، بَسّ اليَوْم مَا أَقْدَر.
  • مُمْكِن مَرّة ثَانِيَة، إن شاء الله.
  • مَتَى؟ السَّاعَة كَم؟ وِين نِلْتَقِي؟
B. Choose the More Natural Dialect Option
  1. “Come to our house tonight”
    A) تَعَال إلى بَيْتِنَا اللَّيْلَة
    B) تَعَال بَيْتْنَا اليَوْم المَسَاء
  2. “I can’t today”
    A) لا أستطيع اليوم
    B) اليَوْم مَا أَقْدَر
  3. “Let’s go”
    A) هَيَّا نَذْهَب
    B) يَاللَّه نَرُوح
Answers
  • 1 → B) تَعَال بَيْتْنَا اليَوْم المَسَاء
  • 2 → B) اليَوْم مَا أَقْدَر
  • 3 → B) يَاللَّه نَرُوح
