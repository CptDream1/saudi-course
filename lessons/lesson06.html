Lesson 6 – Shopping, Prices & Thobe Hunting

Learn how to ask “How much is this?”, talk about money, sizes and colours, and buy thawbs, ʿabāyās, perfume and more – in natural Saudi / Gulf Arabic.

Level: A1 → A2+ Focus: Speaking Topic: Shopping & Prices Time: ~90 minutes

1. Overview – Why Shopping Arabic Matters

Whether you’re in a thobe shop in Birmingham, a perfume shop in Jeddah, or a souq in Saudi / UAE – you will use these phrases all the time.

By the end of this lesson, you can:
  • Ask “How much is this?” and understand basic prices.
  • Ask for sizes (bigger/smaller) and colours.
  • Say you like/don’t like something and decide to buy.
  • Handle a simple price-negotiation politely.
  • Buy a thawb + shemagh combo fully in Arabic.

3. Key Shopping Phrases

These are the lines you’ll use in every clothing / perfume / gift shop.

Asking Price & About Items
  • بكم هذا؟ – bikam hādhā? – How much is this?
  • بكم الثوب؟ – bikam ath-thōb? – How much is the thobe?
  • كم سعره؟ – kam siʿrah? – How much is it? (What’s its price?)
  • عندك مقاس أكبر؟ – ʿindak magās akbar? – Do you have a bigger size?
  • عندك لون ثاني؟ – ʿindak lōn thānī? – Do you have another colour?
Reactions & Opinions
  • غالي شوية – ghālī shwayyah – It’s a bit expensive.
  • رخيص، حلو – rakhīṣ, ḥilw – Cheap, nice.
  • الثوب مرّة حلو – ath-thōb marrah ḥilw – The thobe is really nice.
  • ما عجبني – mā ʿajabnī – I don’t like it.
Buying / Not Buying
  • طيب، آخذه – ṭayyib, ākhudhah – Okay, I’ll take it.
  • بخليه الحين – bakhallīh al-ḥīn – I’ll leave it for now.
  • عندك خصم؟ – ʿindak khaṣm? – Do you have discount?
  • آخر سعر؟ – ākhir siʿr? – Final price?

5. Dialogue – Buying a White Thobe in Birmingham

Realistic Saudi / Gulf shop talk with a Yemeni / Saudi thobe seller.

Arabic
أنت: السلام عليكم.
البائع: وعليكم السلام، هلا والله، تفضل.
أنت: لو سمحت، أبغى ثوب أبيض، أكبر مقاس عندك.
البائع: طيب، شوف هذا… هذا كبير.
أنت: تمام… بكم الثوب؟
البائع: هذا بخمسين جنيه.
أنت: غالي شوية… في خصم؟
البائع: عشانك، خمسة وأربعين.
أنت: طيب، وآخر سعر؟
البائع: والله هذا آخر سعر يا أخوي.
أنت: خلاص، آخذه. عندك شماغ وعقال بعد؟
البائع: أكيد، تفضل، عندي شماغ سعودي أصلي.
Transliteration
anta: as-salāmu ʿalaykum.
al-bāyiʿ: wa-ʿalaykum as-salām, hallā wallah, tfaḍḍal.
anta: law samaḥt, abghā thōb abyaḍ, akbar magās ʿindak.
al-bāyiʿ: ṭayyib, shūf hādhā… hādhā kabīr.
anta: tamām… bikam ath-thōb?
al-bāyiʿ: hādhā b-khamsīn junēh.
anta: ghālī shwayyah… fī khaṣm?
al-bāyiʿ: ʿashānak, khamsah wa-arbaʿīn.
anta: ṭayyib, wa-ākhir siʿr?
al-bāyiʿ: wallah hādhā ākhir siʿr yā akhūy.
anta: khalāṣ, ākhudhah. ʿindak shimāgh wa-ʿigāl baʿd?
al-bāyiʿ: akīd, tfaḍḍal, ʿindī shimāgh saʿūdī aṣlī.
English
You: Peace be upon you.
Seller: And peace be upon you – welcome, come in.
You: Excuse me, I want a white thobe, the biggest size you have.
Seller: Okay, look at this… this one is big.
You: Okay… how much is the thobe?
Seller: This is fifty pounds.
You: A bit expensive… any discount?
Seller: For you – forty-five.
You: Okay – and final price?
Seller: Wallāh, that’s the final price, my brother.
You: Okay, I’ll take it. Do you have a shemagh and ʿigāl as well?
Seller: Of course, here – I have original Saudi shemagh.
Speaking Drill
Read the dialogue out loud 2–3 times. Then change:
  • Item: make it عباية or عطر.
  • Price: change to twenty, thirty, etc.
