Lesson 19 – Eating Out & Restaurants

Ordering like a local in Yemeni, Syrian, Turkish, Saudi and Gulf-style restaurants – asking about the menu, spiciness, portions, and paying the bill.

Level: A2 → early B1 Topic: Restaurants Focus: Ordering & small talk Time: ~90 minutes

1. Overview – When You’ll Use This

Birmingham Yemeni mandi spot, Syrian shawarma, Arab grill in Dubai, hotel buffet in Makkah – this is all that.

By the end of this lesson, you can:
  • Ask for a table, number of people, and see the menu.
  • Order food and drink in natural Najdi/Hijāzi/Gulf-style Arabic.
  • Ask about spiciness, ingredients (e.g. “no mayo”, “no cheese”).
  • Ask for the bill, pay, and compliment the food properly.

3. Getting a Table

  • عَفْوًا، فِيه طَاوِلَة لِأَرْبَعَة؟
    ʿafwan, fīh ṭāwilah li-arbaʿah? – Excuse me, is there a table for four?
  • نِجْلِس هِنَا؟
    nijlis hinā? – Can we sit here?
  • مُمْكِن قَائِمَة الأَكْل؟
    mumkin qā’imat il-akl? – Can we have the menu?
Translate to Arabic
  1. Excuse me, is there a table for two?
  2. Can we sit near the window?
Sample answers

5. Dialogue 1 – Yemeni Restaurant in Birmingham

You and your Yemeni / Arab mates at a mandi place on Coventry Road.

Arabic
المُوَظَّف: مَرْحَبَا، كَم شَخْص؟
أَنْت: أَرْبَعَة، لَو سَمَحْت.
المُوَظَّف: تَفَضَّلُوا، هَذِي طَاوِلَة.
أَنْت: مُمْكِن قَائِمَة الأَكْل؟
المُوَظَّف: أيوه، تْفَضَّل.
أَخُوك: شْرَايْك نَطْلُب مَنْدِي وَمَشَاوِي؟
أَنْت: طَيِّب. أَخُوَي، لَو سَمَحْت!
المُوَظَّف: نِعَم؟
أَنْت: أَبْغَى مَنْدِي لِاثْنَيْن، وَمَشَاوِي لِاثْنَيْن، مَع سَلَطَة وَمِيَّه.
المُوَظَّف: حَارّ وَلاّ نُصّ نُصّ؟
أَنْت: نُصّ نُصّ، مُو كَثِير حَارّ، الله يِسْلِمَك.
Transliteration
al-muwaẓẓaf: marḥabā, kam shakhs?
anta: arbaʿah, law samaḥt.
al-muwaẓẓaf: tafaddalū, hādhī ṭāwilah.
anta: mumkin qā’imat il-akl?
al-muwaẓẓaf: aywah, tfaddal.
akhūk: shrāyk naṭlub mandī wa mashāwī?
anta: ṭayyib. akhūy, law samaḥt!
al-muwaẓẓaf: niʿam?
anta: abghā mandī li-ithnēn, wa mashāwī li-ithnēn, maʿ salatah wa miyyah.
al-muwaẓẓaf: ḥārr wallā nuṣṣ nuṣṣ?
anta: nuṣṣ nuṣṣ, mū kathīr ḥārr, Allāh yislimak.
English
Staff: Welcome, how many people?
You: Four, please.
Staff: Come, this table here.
You: Can we have the menu?
Staff: Yes, here you go.
Your brother: What do you think, shall we order mandi and grill?
You: Okay. Brother, excuse me!
Staff: Yes?
You: I want mandi for two, and mixed grill for two, with salad and water.
Staff: Spicy or medium?
You: Medium, not too spicy, may Allah keep you safe.

6. Complimenting the Food & Small Complaints

6.1 Praising the Food

  • الأَكْل مَرّة لَذِيذ – il-akl marrah ladhīdh – the food is really tasty
  • المَنْدِي خُرَافِي – il-mandī khurāfī – the mandi is unreal
  • يَعْطِيكُم العَافِيَة – yaʿṭīkum il-ʿāfiyah – may Allah give you strength (for your effort)

6.2 Light Complaints (Polite)

  • مَعْلِيش، الأَكْل بَارِد شَوَيّ – maʿlēsh, il-akl bārid shwayy – sorry, the food is a bit cold
  • مُمْكِن تْزِيد الشَّطَّة شَوَيّ؟ – mumkin tzīd ash-shaṭṭah shwayy? – can you add a bit more chilli?
  • طَعْمُه مَالِح شَوَيّ – ṭaʿmuh māliḥ shwayy – it tastes a bit salty

7. Asking for the Bill & Paying

  • الحِسَاب، لَو سَمَحْت – il-ḥisāb, law samaḥt – the bill please
  • نِقْسِم الحِسَاب نِصّ نِصّ – nigsim il-ḥisāb niṣṣ niṣṣ – we’ll split the bill 50/50
  • أَدْفَع أَنَا – adfaʿ anā – I will pay
Mini Practice
  1. Say: “The bill please.”
  2. Say: “I’ll pay.”
  3. Say: “We’ll split the bill.”
  • الحِسَاب، لَو سَمَحْت.
  • أَدْفَع أَنَا.
  • نِقْسِم الحِسَاب نِصّ نِصّ.

9. End-of-Lesson Quiz

A. Translate to Arabic
  1. Excuse me, is there a table for three?
  2. I want one shawarma and one mixed grill, not too spicy.
  3. The food is very tasty, may Allah give you strength.
  4. The food is a bit salty.
  5. The bill, please.
Sample answers
  • عَفْوًا، فِيه طَاوِلَة لِثَلَاثَة؟
  • أَبْغَى شَاوِرْمَا وَاحِدَة وَمَشَاوِي وَاحِدَة، مُو كَثِير حَارّ.
  • الأَكْل مَرّة لَذِيذ، يَعْطِيكُم العَافِيَة.
  • طَعْمُه مَالِح شَوَيّ.
  • الحِسَاب، لَو سَمَحْت.
B. Choose the More Natural Dialect Option
  1. “The bill please”
    A) أُرِيدُ الفَاتُورَة مِن فَضْلِكَ
    B) الحِسَاب، لَو سَمَحْت
  2. “The food is very tasty”
    A) الطَّعَام لَذِيذ جِدًّا
    B) الأَكْل مَرّة لَذِيذ
  3. “Not too spicy”
    A) لَيْسَ حَارًّا جِدًّا
    B) مُو كَثِير حَارّ
Answers
  • 1 → B) الحِسَاب، لَو سَمَحْت
  • 2 → B) الأَكْل مَرّة لَذِيذ
  • 3 → B) مُو كَثِير حَارّ
