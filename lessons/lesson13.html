Lesson 13 – Future & “Gonna” Talk

Say “I’m gonna go”, “We’ll eat later”, “Tomorrow I’ll study”, “On Friday we’re going ʿUmrah” – in Saudi / Gulf style.

Level: A2 → early B1 Grammar: Future Focus: Speaking & Plans Time: ~90 minutes

1. Overview – How Saudis Say “Gonna”

English has “will / gonna / planning to”. Saudi / Gulf Arabic usually uses:

  • رَاح + present verb – rāḥ arūḥ, rāḥ nṛūḥ… (more Gulf / Hijāzi)
  • بَـ + present verb – barūḥ, banākol… (Hijāzi / Gulf)
  • أَبْغَى + present – abghā arūḥ… (more like “I want to go” but often used as future)

We’ll mainly use **رَاح** and **بَـ** because they’re super common and sound natural in your Najdi/Hijāzi/Gulf blend.

By the end of this lesson, you can:
  • Use رَاح and بَـ to talk about the future.
  • Say what you’ll do tomorrow, next week, on Friday, in Ramaḍān, etc.
  • Make and accept/refuse invitations (“we’re gonna go”, “I can’t come”).
  • Talk about near future plans for ʿUmrah / Saudi / Arab world trips.

2. Core Future Patterns

Memorise these “shapes” then swap verbs/words inside.

2.1 رَاح + Present Verb

رَاح + [present tense] ≈ “will / gonna”

  • رَاح أَرُوح – rāḥ arūḥ – I’ll go / I’m gonna go
  • رَاح نَرُوح – rāḥ narūḥ – we’ll go
  • رَاح آكُل – rāḥ ākul – I’ll eat
  • رَاح تَدْرُس – rāḥ tadrus – he/she will study (context)

2.2 بَـ + Present Verb (especially Hijāzi/Gulf)

بَـ + [present verb] ≈ “gonna”

  • بَرُوح – barūḥ – I’m gonna go
  • بَنرُوح – banrūḥ – we’re gonna go
  • بَآكُل – bākul – I’m gonna eat
  • بَنَدْرُس – bandrus – we’re gonna study

You can use either pattern. A nice combo for you: رَاح when you speak a bit slower / clearer, and بَـ when you want to sound more Hijāzi/Gulf/casual.

3. Sample Sentences – Plug & Play

All of these use verbs and vocab you already know.

  • بَكْرَه رَاح أَرُوح المَسْجِد بَدْرِي – bukrah rāḥ arūḥ il-masjid badrī – Tomorrow I’ll go to the masjid early.
  • اليَوْم المَسَاء بَنَأكُل بَرَّا – il-yōm il-masā banākol barra – Tonight we’re gonna eat out.
  • رَاح أَدْرُس شُوَيَّة بَعْدِين أَشُوف نَتْفْلِكْس – rāḥ adrus shwayyah baʿdēn ashūf Netflix – I’ll study a bit then watch Netflix.
  • في رَمَضَان بَنِحَاوِل نَرُوح التَّرَاوِيح كُل يَوْم – fī Ramaḍān banḥāwil narūḥ at-tarāwīḥ kull yōm – In Ramaḍān we’ll try to go to tarāwīḥ every day.
Your Turn – English → Arabic
Write in your notebook, using رَاح or بَـ:
  1. Tomorrow I’m going to study Arabic.
  2. Tonight we’re going to eat kabsa.
  3. On Friday we’ll go to the masjid early.
Sample answers
  • بَكْرَه رَاح أَدْرُس عَرَبِي – bukrah rāḥ adrus ʿArabī
  • اليَوْم المَسَاء بَنَأكُل كَبْسَة – il-yōm il-masā banākol kabsa
  • الجُمُعَة رَاح نَرُوح المَسْجِد بَدْرِي – al-jumʿah rāḥ narūḥ il-masjid badrī

4. Negative Future – “I’m Not Gonna…”

You already know ما. Just bring it to the future.

Two very common patterns:

  • ما رَاح + present – I’m not gonna / I won’t
  • ما بَـ + present – I’m not gonna (more casual)
  • ما رَاح أَرُوح اليَوْم – mā rāḥ arūḥ il-yōm – I’m not going today.
  • ما بَرُوح الحَفْلَة – mā barūḥ il-ḥaflah – I’m not gonna go to the party.
  • ما رَاح نَأكُل بَرَّا – mā rāḥ nākul barra – We’re not gonna eat out.
Practice – Make It Negative
Turn these into “not gonna”:
  1. بَرُوح المَسْجِد – I’m gonna go to the masjid.
  2. بَنَأكُل بَرَّا – We’re gonna eat out.
  3. رَاح أَدْرُس اليَوْم – I’ll study today.
Answers
  • ما بَرُوح المَسْجِد
  • ما رَاح نَأكُل بَرَّا
  • ما رَاح أَدْرُس اليَوْم

6. Dialogue 1 – “What Are You Doing Tomorrow?”

Classic chat with a Saudi / Yemeni / Syrian friend in Birmingham.

Arabic
صَاحِبَك: بَكْرَه إيش رَاح تِسَوِّي؟
أَنْت: بَكْرَه الصُّبْح بَرُوح الشُّغْل، بَعْدِين بَرُوح المَسْجِد.
صَاحِبَك: وبَعْد المَغْرِب؟
أَنْت: بَعْد المَغْرِب رَاح أَدْرُس عَرَبِي شُوَيَّة، وبَعْدِين أَرِيَّح.
صَاحِبَك: حلو، الله يوفّقك يا رجل.
Transliteration
ṣāḥibak: bukrah ēsh rāḥ tisawwī?
anta: bukrah aṣ-ṣubḥ barūḥ ash-shughol, baʿdēn barūḥ il-masjid.
ṣāḥibak: w-baʿd al-maghrib?
anta: baʿd al-maghrib rāḥ adrus ʿArabī shwayyah, w-baʿdēn arayyaḥ.
ṣāḥibak: ḥilw, Allāh ywaffigak yā rajul.
English
Your friend: What are you going to do tomorrow?
You: Tomorrow morning I’m going to work, then I’ll go to the masjid.
Your friend: And after Maghrib?
You: After Maghrib I’ll study Arabic a bit, then I’ll rest.
Your friend: Nice, may Allah give you success, man.
Personalise It
Change:
  • “work” → your real schedule (college / madrasa / day off).
  • “study Arabic” → something you actually plan (gym / Qur’ān / driving lesson).

7. Dialogue 2 – Planning ʿUmrah

This one is literally your real life in shā’ Allah.

Arabic
أَبُوك: إن شاء الله الشَّهْر الجَاي رَاح نَرُوح عُمْرَة.
أَنْت: صَدْق؟ والله خَبَر حِلُو! مَتَى رَاح نَسَافِر؟
أَبُوك: بَعْد رَمَضَان بَكْرَهِين، نَحْجِز التَّذَاكِر الأُسْبُوع الجَاي.
أَنْت: طَيِّب، رَاح أَجَهِّز الشُّنْطَة بَدْرِي.
أَبُوك: ورَاح نِقَعُد فِي مَكَّة يَوْمِين، وبَعْدِين نَرُوح المَدِينَة.
Transliteration
abūk: inshāʼ Allāh ash-shahr il-jāy rāḥ narūḥ ʿumrah.
anta: ṣidg? wallah khabar ḥilw! matā rāḥ nasāfir?
abūk: baʿd Ramaḍān b-shwayyah, naḥjiz at-tadhāker al-usbūʿ il-jāy.
anta: ṭayyib, rāḥ ajahhiz ash-shunṭah badrī.
abūk: wa rāḥ nigaʿʿud fī Makkah yōmayn, w-baʿdēn narūḥ il-Madīnah.
English
Your dad: In shā’ Allah next month we’re going for ʿUmrah.
You: Really? That’s such nice news! When are we going to travel?
Your dad: After Ramaḍān by a bit, we’ll book the tickets next week.
You: Okay, I’ll prepare the suitcase early.
Your dad: And we’ll stay in Makkah two days, then go to Madīnah.

10. End-of-Lesson Quiz

A. Translate to Arabic (Future)
  1. Tomorrow I’ll go to the masjid.
  2. Tonight we’re going to eat outside.
  3. Next week we’ll start a new Arabic lesson.
  4. I’m not going to work tomorrow.
  5. In the future I’ll work in the Gulf, in shā’ Allah.
Sample answers
  • بَكْرَه رَاح أَرُوح المَسْجِد.
  • اليَوْم المَسَاء بَنَأكُل بَرَّا.
  • الأُسْبُوع الجَاي بَنِبْدَأ دَرْس جَدِيد عَرَبِي.
  • بَكْرَه ما رَاح أَرُوح الشُّغْل.
  • في المُسْتَقْبَل رَاح أَشْتَغِل في الخَلِيج، إن شاء الله.
B. Choose the More Natural Dialect Option
  1. “I’m going to eat”:
    A) سآكل
    B) بَآكُل
  2. “We will go to the masjid”:
    A) سوف نذهب إلى المسجد
    B) رَاح نَرُوح المَسْجِد
  3. “I’m not going to go”:
    A) لن أذهب
    B) ما رَاح أَرُوح
Answers
  • 1 → B) بَآكُل
  • 2 → B) رَاح نَرُوح المَسْجِد
  • 3 → B) ما رَاح أَرُوح
