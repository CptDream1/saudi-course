Lesson 32 – Survival in Any Arab Country

How to survive and function in Saudi, Egypt, Yemen, Levant, Gulf, Sudan, Maghreb, etc. With only your Najdi/Hijāzi/Gulf mix + a few smart “universal Arabic” phrases.

Level: Strong A2 → B1 Focus: Survival + clarity Time: ~90 minutes

1️⃣ Overview – What “Survival” Means

You’re not trying to sound Egyptian, Syrian, Moroccan etc. You just need:

  • To be understood when you need something.
  • To understand the gist of what they reply.
  • To control the speed and ask for repetition.
  • To explain “I speak some Arabic, please keep it simple”.

Your dialect = Saudi/Gulf base + simple MSA-ish vocabulary when needed.

2️⃣ Universal Courtesy Phrases

Works with basically *any* Arab nationality.

Arabic Translit Meaning
لو سَمَحْت law samaḥt please / excuse me (to a man)
لو سَمَحْتِي law samaḥtī please (to a woman)
مَعْلِيش maʿlēsh sorry / don’t mind
مُمْكِن…؟ mumkin…? is it possible…? / can I…?
شُكْرًا جَزِيلًا shukran jazīlan thank you very much
الله يَجْزِيك خَيْر Allāh yijzīk khayr may Allah reward you with good
Drill

Say each one out loud 5×. Mix them into your sentences in later sections.

3️⃣ Controlling the Conversation: “I Only Speak a Bit”

This is your superpower line. Use it at the beginning.

Arabic Translit Meaning
أَتْكَلَّم عَرَبِي شُوَيّ شُوَيّ atkallam ʿarabī shwayy shwayy I speak a little Arabic
كَلِّمْنِي بُطِّيء شْوَيّ، لو سمحت kallimnī baṭṭī’ shwayy, law samaḥt Speak to me a bit slowly, please
إِذَا مَا فَهِمْت، أَقُول لَك idhā mā fihimt, agūl lak If I don’t understand, I’ll tell you
Survival Script

Memorise this mini intro:

السَّلام عليكم، أَتْكَلَّم عَرَبِي شُوَيّ شُوَيّ، كَلِّمْنِي بُطِّيء شْوَيّ لو سَمَحْت.
as-salāmu ʿalaykum, atkallam ʿarabī shwayy shwayy, kallimnī baṭṭī’ shwayy law samaḥt.

4️⃣ Asking for Meaning / Repeating

Arabic Translit Meaning
شُو مَعْنَى هَذَا؟ shū maʿnā hādhā? What does this mean?
أَعِيد، لو سَمَحْت aʿīd, law samaḥt Say it again please
شْوَيّ أَبْطَأ shwayy abṭa’ A bit slower
فِيه كَلِمَة مَا فَهِمْتْهَا fīh kalimah mā fahimt-hā There is a word I didn’t understand
Mini Drill

Combine:

  • أَتْكَلَّم عَرَبِي شُوَيّ شُوَيّ
  • أَعِيد، لو سمحت
  • شُو مَعْنَى هَذَا؟

5️⃣ Money, Taxi & Hotel – Core Survival Domain

5.1 Asking Prices & Paying

ArabicTranslitMeaning
بِكَم هَذَا؟ bikam hādhā? How much is this?
غَالِي شْوَيّ ghālī shwayy It’s a bit expensive
فِيه خَصْم؟ fīh khaṣm? Is there a discount?
عِنْدَك فَكَّة؟ ʿindak fakkah? Do you have change?

5.2 Taxi / Uber

ArabicTranslitMeaning
تَكْسِي / أُوبِر taksī / ūbar taxi / Uber
أَبْغَى أَرُوح… abghā arūḥ… I want to go to…
وَقِّف هِنَا، لو سَمَحْت waqqif hinā, law samaḥt Stop here please
بِكَم لِلمَكَان؟ bikam lil-makān? How much to this place?

5.3 Hotel Check-in

ArabicTranslitMeaning
عِنْدِي حَجْز ʿindī ḥajz I have a booking
غُرْفَة لِشَخْص وَاحِد ghurfah li shakhṣ wāḥid Room for 1 person
بِالفُطُور وَلّا بِدُون؟ bil-fuṭūr walla bidūn? With breakfast or without?
فِيه وَاي فَاي؟ fīh wifi? Is there WiFi?
Translate
  1. How much is this? It’s a bit expensive.
  2. I want to go to the hotel, stop here please.
  3. I have a booking, a room for one person.
Sample answers
  • بِكَم هَذَا؟ غَالِي شْوَيّ.
  • أَبْغَى أَرُوح الفُنْدُق، وَقِّف هِنَا لو سَمَحْت.
  • عِنْدِي حَجْز، غُرْفَة لِشَخْص وَاحِد.

6️⃣ Emergencies & Problems

In shā’ Allāh you never need this, but better to know.

ArabicTranslitMeaning
سَاعِدْنِي، لو سمحت sāʿidnī, law samaḥt Help me, please
فَقَدْت جَوَّالِي faqadt jawwālī I lost my phone
فَقَدْت الجَوَاز / البَاسْبُور faqadt il-jawāz / il-bāsbūr I lost my passport
أَنَا مَرِيض anā marīḍ I’m ill
أَبْغَى مُسْتَشْفَى abghā mustashfā I need a hospital
Emergency Script

Memorise this:

سَاعِدْنِي لو سَمَحْت، فَقَدْت جَوَّالِي، أَتْكَلَّم عَرَبِي شُوَيّ شُوَيّ.
sāʿidnī law samaḥt, faqadt jawwālī, atkallam ʿarabī shwayy shwayy.

7️⃣ Micro Dialogues – Different Arab Countries

You speak in your Saudi/Gulf mix, they reply in their dialect.

7.1 In Cairo (Egypt)

أَنْت: السَّلام عليكم، أَتْكَلَّم عَرَبِي شُوَيّ شُوَيّ، أَبْغَى تَكْسِي لِلهُوتِيل.
مِصْرِي: وعليكم السلام يا بِيه، مَشْكِلَة، مَفِيش مُشْكِلَة، تْفَضَّل.
أَنْت: بِكَم لِلهُوتِيل؟ غَالِي شْوَيّ.
مِصْرِي: لَأ يَا رِيس، دَه سِعْر حِلُو.

7.2 In Amman (Jordan / Levant)

أَنْت: لو سَمَحْت، أَبْغَى أَرُوح المَسْجِد.
شَامِي: تْفَضَّل أَخُوي، المَسْجِد هُنَاك عَلي يَمِين.
أَنْت: شُكْرًا جَزِيلًا، الله يَجْزِيك خَيْر.
شَامِي: وِيَّاك، الله يِسْهِّل.

7.3 In Dubai / Doha (Gulf)

أَنْت: السَّلام عليكم، أَبْغَى غُرْفَة لِشَخْص وَاحِد، لَيْلَتَيْن.
خَلِيجِي: وعليكم السَّلام، مَا فِي مُشْكِلَة، فِيه غُرْفَة جَاهِزَة.
أَنْت: فِيه وَاي فَاي؟
خَلِيجِي: إِيه، فِيه وَاي فَاي مَجَّانِي.

8️⃣ End-of-Lesson Quiz – Are You Surviving?

A. Translate to Arabic
  1. Please, I only speak a little Arabic.
  2. Speak slowly, please.
  3. How much is this? It’s a bit expensive.
  4. I lost my passport, help me please.
  5. I have a booking, a room with breakfast.
Sample answers
  • لو سَمَحْت، أَتْكَلَّم عَرَبِي شُوَيّ شُوَيّ.
  • كَلِّمْنِي بُطِّيء شْوَيّ، لو سَمَحْت.
  • بِكَم هَذَا؟ غَالِي شْوَيّ.
  • فَقَدْت الجَوَاز، سَاعِدْنِي لو سَمَحْت.
  • عِنْدِي حَجْز، غُرْفَة مَع الفُطُور.
If you can do this lesson comfortably, you can survive in almost any Arab city using your Najdi/Hijāzi/Gulf base + a few universal phrases.
