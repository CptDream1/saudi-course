Lesson 7 – Directions & Transport

Ask “Where is the masjid?”, “How do I get there?”, talk to taxi/Uber drivers, and not get lost – in Riyadh, Jeddah, Makkah, or Birmingham – using natural Saudi / Gulf Arabic.

Level: A1 → A2+ Focus: Speaking & Listening Topic: Directions & Transport Time: ~90 minutes

1. Overview – Not Getting Lost in Arabic

If you can handle “left, right, straight, near, far, where is…”, you can survive almost any city with Arabs – and have good small talk with drivers.

By the end of this lesson, you can:
  • Ask where places are and understand basic directions.
  • Say near/far, left/right, straight, traffic lights, roundabout.
  • Tell a taxi/Uber driver where to go and when to stop.
  • Talk about how you usually go to work/masjid (car, bus, walking).
  • Handle “Umrah taxi / Haram bus” situations at a basic level.

4. Key Phrases – “Where Is…?” & “How Do I Get There?”

These are the core questions to survive any city as a beginner.

Where is…?
  • وين المسجد؟ – wēn al-masjid? – Where is the masjid?
  • وين أقرب مسجد؟ – wēn aqrab masjid? – Where is the nearest masjid?
  • وين محطة الباص؟ – wēn maḥaṭṭat al-bāṣ? – Where is the bus station?
  • وين الحرم؟ – wēn al-Ḥaram? – Where is the Ḥaram?
How do I go to…?
  • كيف أروح الحرم؟ – kēf arūḥ al-Ḥaram? – How do I go to the Ḥaram?
  • كيف أروح المول؟ – kēf arūḥ al-mōl? – How do I go to the mall?
Giving Simple Directions
  • روح على طول – rūḥ ʿalā ṭūl – Go straight.
  • بعدين لفّ يمين – baʿdēn liff yamīn – Then turn right.
  • بعدين لفّ يسار – baʿdēn liff yasār – Then turn left.
  • المسجد جنب المول – al-masjid janb al-mōl – The masjid is next to the mall.
  • قريب من البيت – qarīb min al-bayt – It’s near the house.
  • بعيد شوية – baʿīd shwayyah – It’s a bit far.

5. Dialogue 1 – Asking for the Masjid

Realistic simple street conversation with a random uncle in Birmingham or Jeddah.

Arabic
أنت: السلام عليكم يا عم.
العم: وعليكم السلام يا حبيب، تفضل.
أنت: لو سمحت، وين أقرب مسجد؟
العم: شوف، روح على طول، بعدين لفّ يمين.
أنت: بعيد؟
العم: لا لا، قريب، خمس دقايق مشي.
أنت: جزاك الله خير، يعطيك العافية.
العم: وإيّاك يا أخوي.
Transliteration
anta: as-salāmu ʿalaykum yā ʿamm.
al-ʿamm: wa-ʿalaykum as-salām yā ḥabīb, tfaḍḍal.
anta: law samaḥt, wēn aqrab masjid?
al-ʿamm: shūf, rūḥ ʿalā ṭūl, baʿdēn liff yamīn.
anta: baʿīd?
al-ʿamm: lā lā, qarīb, khams dagāyiʿ mashy.
anta: jazāk Allāh khayr, yaʿṭīk al-ʿāfiyah.
al-ʿamm: wa-iyyāk yā akhūy.
English
You: Peace be upon you, uncle.
Uncle: And peace be upon you, my dear – go ahead.
You: Excuse me, where is the nearest masjid?
Uncle: Look, go straight, then turn right.
You: Is it far?
Uncle: No, no – it’s near, five minutes walking.
You: May Allah reward you with good, may Allah give you wellness.
Uncle: And you too, my brother.
Speaking Drill
Read the dialogue out loud 2–3 times. Then change:
  • Place: “masjid” → “mall” or “train station”.
  • Distance: “five minutes” → “ten minutes by car”.

6. Dialogue 2 – Taxi / Uber to the Masjid

Basic, realistic talk with an Arab driver in Saudi, UAE, or Birmingham.

Arabic
السائق: السلام عليكم.
أنت: وعليكم السلام، لو سمحت…
السائق: وين تروح؟
أنت: أبغى أروح المسجد الكبير في المركز.
السائق: تمام، تعرف الطريق؟
أنت: شوي… روح على طول، بعدين لفّ يسار عند الإشارة.
السائق: طيب، إذا قربنا قول لي “هنا”.
أنت: إن شاء الله، شكراً.
السائق: العفو.
Transliteration
as-sāʼiq: as-salāmu ʿalaykum.
anta: wa-ʿalaykum as-salām, law samaḥt…
as-sāʼiq: wēn trūḥ?
anta: abghā arūḥ al-masjid al-kabīr fil-markaz.
as-sāʼiq: tamām, taʿrif aṭ-ṭarīq?
anta: shway… rūḥ ʿalā ṭūl, baʿdēn liff yasār ʿind al-ishārah.
as-sāʼiq: ṭayyib, itha garabnā gūl lī “hnā”.
anta: inshāʼ Allāh, shukran.
as-sāʼiq: al-ʿafw.
English
Driver: Peace be upon you.
You: And peace be upon you, excuse me…
Driver: Where are you going?
You: I want to go to the big masjid in the centre.
Driver: Okay, do you know the way?
You: A bit… go straight, then turn left at the traffic lights.
Driver: Okay, when we get close, say to me “here”.
You: In shā’ Allāh, thanks.
Driver: You’re welcome.
Speaking Drill
Practise saying “Stop here” / “Go a bit more” in Arabic:
  • وقف هنا لو سمحت – waqqif hnā law samaḥt – Stop here please.
  • كمل شوية – kammil shwayyah – Go a little more.
