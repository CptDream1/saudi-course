Lesson 3 – Daily Routine & Daily Life Arabic

Talk about your normal day in clear Saudi / Gulf Arabic – waking up, praying, work, study, chilling – and ask others about their routine.

Level: A1 → A2 Boost Focus: Speaking & Listening Topic: Daily Routine Time: ~90 minutes

1. Overview – Why Daily Routine Matters

If you can describe your day and ask about someone else’s, you can start real conversations with Arabs anywhere – masjid, café, work, uni, even in the thobe shop.

By the end of this lesson, you can:
  • Say what time you wake up, sleep, pray, work or study.
  • Use present tense dialect verbs naturally (I go / you go / we go).
  • Describe your daily routine from Fajr to sleep.
  • Ask Arabs about their routine and understand the answer.
  • Handle masjid + work + home small talk about “your day”.

2. Core Vocabulary – My Day (Najdi / Hijāzi / Gulf)

Learn these first. They will appear in almost every routine sentence.

أتعشّى atʿashshā I eat dinner From ʿashā – dinner
أروح arūḥ I go Core Saudi / Gulf verb
أرجع arjaʿ I return / come back All dialects

3. Present Tense – Dialect Pattern

In Gulf / Saudi Arabic, present tense is easy: just small changes at the beginning of the verb.

Example Verb: يروح – “to go”
English Arabic Translit
I go أروح arūḥ
You (m) go تروح trūḥ
You (f) go تروحي trūḥī
He goes يروح yrūḥ
She goes تروح trūḥ
We go نروح nrūḥ

You’ll see these prefixes a lot: a- (I), t- (you/she), y- (he), n- (we).

Mini Practice – Fill the Gaps
Complete with the correct form of يروح (to go):
  1. أنا ______ المسجد المغرب. (I go to the masjid at Maghrib.)
  2. إنتَ ______ الشغل الصباح. (You (m) go to work in the morning.)
  3. هيّ ______ المدرسة كل يوم. (She goes to school every day.)
  4. إحنا ______ الجامعة. (We go to uni.)
Answers
  1. أنا أروح المسجد المغرب.
  2. إنتَ تروح الشغل الصباح.
  3. هيّ تروح المدرسة كل يوم.
  4. إحنا نروح الجامعة.

4. Sentence Pattern – WHO + ACTION + WHEN/WHERE

We expand your A–B–C formula from Lesson 1 for daily routine.

Think:

  • A = WHO? (I / you / he / she…)
  • B = ACTION? (wake up / pray / go / work…)
  • C = WHEN / WHERE? (in the morning / at night / to the masjid…)
Examples
أنا أصحى الصباح وأصلّي الفجر
ana aṣḥā aṣ-ṣabāḥ wa-aṣalli al-fajr – I wake up in the morning and pray Fajr.
إنت تروح الشغل كل يوم
inta trūḥ ash-shughol kull yōm – You go to work every day.
هوّ يرجع البيت العصر
huwwa yarjaʿ il-bayt al-ʿaṣr – He goes back home in the afternoon.
إحنا نتعشّى سوا بالليل
iḥnā nitʿashshā sawā bil-lēl – We eat dinner together at night.
Your Turn – Translate
Using only vocab from Lessons 1–3, translate:
  1. I wake up in the morning.
  2. She goes to the masjid at Maghrib.
  3. We eat dinner at home.
  4. You (m) sleep early.
Sample answers
  • أنا أصحى الصباح.
  • هيّ تروح المسجد المغرب.
  • إحنا نتعشّى في البيت.
  • إنت تنام بدري.

5. Dialogue – “What’s Your Day Like?”

A realistic Saudi/Gulf-style conversation about daily life.

Arabic
أ: متى تصحى كل يوم؟
ب: أصحى الصباح، حوالي الساعة سبعة.
أ: وبعدين؟
ب: أتوضّى، أصلّي الفجر، وبعدين أروح الشغل.
أ: ترجع البيت متى؟
ب: أرجع العصر تقريبًا، أتغدّى مع الأهل، وبعدين أرتاح شوي.
أ: وبالليل؟
ب: أتعشّى، أشوف شوية فيديوهات، وبعدين أنام.
Transliteration
A: mitā tiṣḥā kull yōm?
B: aṣḥā aṣ-ṣabāḥ, ḥawālī as-sāʿah sabʿah.
A: w-baʿdēn?
B: atwaḍḍa, aṣalli al-fajr, w-baʿdēn arūḥ ash-shughol.
A: tirjaʿ il-bayt mitā?
B: arjaʿ al-ʿaṣr taqrīban, atghaddā maʿ al-ahl, w-baʿdēn artāḥ shwayy.
A: w-bil-lēl?
B: atʿashshā, ashūf shwayyat fīdiyohāt, w-baʿdēn anām.
English
A: When do you wake up every day?
B: I wake up in the morning, around seven o’clock.
A: And then?
B: I make wudū’, pray Fajr, then I go to work.
A: When do you come back home?
B: I come back around ʿAṣr, eat lunch with the family, then rest a bit.
A: And at night?
B: I eat dinner, watch some videos, then sleep.
Speaking Drill
Read the dialogue out loud 3 times. Then change it to be about your real day.
  1. Say it exactly as written.
  2. Change the times (e.g. “الساعة تسعة”).
  3. Change “الشغل” to “الجامعة” or “المدرسة”.

6. Write Your Routine (Short Paragraph)

Now make it personal – this is the Arabic you’ll actually use.

Task
In your notebook, write 4–6 sentences in Arabic about your real daily routine. Use these ideas:
  • What time you wake up.
  • If you pray Fajr in the masjid or at home.
  • Where you go (work / uni / madrasa / home).
  • What you do in the afternoon.
  • What time you sleep.
Sample paragraph
أنا أصحى الصباح حوالي الساعة ثمانية. أتوضّى وأصلّي الفجر في البيت. بعدين أروح الجامعة وأدرس لحدّ العصر. أرجع البيت، أتغدّى مع أهلي، وبالليل أتعشّى، أشوف شوية فيديوهات عربية، وبعدين أنام بدري.
ana aṣḥa aṣ-ṣabāḥ ḥawālī as-sāʿah thamāniyah. atwaḍḍa wa-aṣalli al-fajr fil-bayt. baʿdēn arūḥ al-jāmiʿah wa-adrus li-ḥadd al-ʿaṣr. arjaʿ il-bayt, atghaddā maʿ ahlī, w-bil-lēl atʿashshā, ashūf shwayyat fīdiyohāt ʿarabiyyah, w-baʿdēn anām badrī.

  2. “We eat dinner at home”
