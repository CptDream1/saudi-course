Lesson 14 – Opinions & Preferences

Say what you like, don’t like, love, hate – and give your opinion without sounding rude or robotic.

Level: A2 → early B1 Focus: Conversation Topic: Likes, dislikes & opinions Time: ~90 minutes

1. Overview – Why This Lesson Matters

Real friendships in Arabic start when you can say “I like this, I don’t like that, I think…”.

By the end of this lesson, you can:
  • Say “I like / I love / I don’t like / I hate” naturally in Saudi-style Arabic.
  • Give simple opinions: “I think…”, “In my opinion…”, “For me…”.
  • Talk about your preferences in food, places, people, routines.
  • Agree and disagree politely with Arab friends.

2. Core Verbs – Like / Love / Don't Like / Hate

These will come up every day with Saudis, Yemenis, Syrians, etc.

English Arabic Translit Notes
I like أَحِبّ aḥibb most common, universal
I love (strong like) أَعْشَق / مَرّة أَحِبّ aʿshag / marrah aḥibb casual “love it” feeling
I don’t like ما أَحِبّ mā aḥibb soft negative, polite
I hate / really don’t like أَكْرَه akrah strong; use carefully
It’s nice حِلُو ḥilw literally “sweet”; very common
It’s not nice مُو حِلُو / مَب حِلُو mū ḥilw / mab ḥilw Gulf/Saudi “not nice”
  • أَحِبّ القَهْوَة – aḥibb il-gahwah – I like coffee.
  • ما أَحِبّ الزَّحْمَة – mā aḥibb az-zaḥmah – I don’t like crowds.
  • الأَكْل هَذَا مَرّة حِلُو – al-akl hādhā marrah ḥilw – This food is really nice.

3. Preferences – Food & Drink

You’ll use these with Arab aunties, uncles, family friends, and in restaurants.

3.1 Useful vocab

ArabicTranslitMeaning
كَبْسَةkabsakabsa (Saudi rice dish)
مَنْدِيmandīmundi (Yemeni style)
شَايshāytea
قَهْوَة عَرَبِيّةgahwah ʿarabiyyahArabic coffee
حَارّḥārhot / spicy (context)
مُبَرِّد / بَارِدmubarrid / bāridcold
حَلُوḥalūsweet (taste)
مَالِحmāliḥsalty

3.2 Example sentences

  • أَحِبّ الكَبْسَة أَكْثَر مِن المَنْدِي – aḥibb il-kabsa akthar min il-mandī – I like kabsa more than mandi.
  • ما أَحِبّ الأَكْل المَرّة حَارّ – mā aḥibb al-akl il-marrah ḥār – I don’t like food that’s too spicy.
  • أَحِبّ الشَّاي أَكْثَر مِن القَهْوَة – aḥibb ash-shāy akthar min al-gahwah – I like tea more than coffee.
Practice – Your Food Preferences
Write in Arabic (or Arabic + translit) in your notebook:
  1. I like ______ more than ______.
  2. I don’t like very spicy food.
  3. Arabic coffee is nice, but tea is better for me.
Sample answers
  • أَحِبّ المَنْدِي أَكْثَر مِن الكَبْسَة.
  • ما أَحِبّ الأَكْل المَرّة حَارّ.
  • القَهْوَة العَرَبِيّة حِلُوَة، بَسّ الشَّاي أَحْسَن لِي.

4. Saying “I Think / I Feel / In My Opinion”

These make your Arabic sound grown-up and less robotic.

ArabicTranslitMeaning / Use
أَشُوف إِنُّو... ashūf innu… I think (literally “I see that…”)
أَحِسّ إِنُّو... aḥiss innu… I feel (that)…
فِي رَأْيِي... fī ra’yī… in my opinion… (slightly more formal)
وَالله أَشُوف... wallah ashūf… to soften: “honestly I think…”

Examples

  • أَشُوف إِنُّو المَدِينَة أَهْدَى مِن مَكَّة – ashūf innu il-Madīnah ahdā min Makkah – I think Madīnah is calmer than Makkah.
  • أَحِسّ إِنُّو العَرَبِي صَعْب شُوَيَّة، بَسّ حِلُو – aḥiss innu il-ʿArabī ṣaʿb shwayyah, bass ḥilw – I feel that Arabic is a bit hard, but nice.
  • وَالله أَشُوف الدَّرْس اليَوْم مَفِيد – wallah ashūf ad-dars il-yōm mafīd – Honestly, I think today’s lesson is useful.

5. Softening Your Opinion (Polite Arab Style)

Arabs often soften negatives; it sounds more respectful.

ArabicTranslitMeaning / Function
شُوَيَّة shwayyah a bit
مَرّة marrah very / really (Hijāzi/Gulf)
مُو مَرّة mū marrah not really
صَرَاحَةً ṣarāḥatan honestly
شُوَيّ صَعْب shwayy ṣaʿb a bit hard
  • صَرَاحَةً ما أَحِبّ هَذَا المَطْعَم، الأَكْل مُو مَرّة – ṣarāḥatan mā aḥibb hādhā il-maṭʿam, al-akl mū marrah – Honestly, I don’t like this restaurant, the food isn’t really great.
  • الدَّرْس شُوَيّ صَعْب – ad-dars shwayy ṣaʿb – The lesson is a bit hard.

6. Dialogue 1 – Talking About Food & Places

Imagine you and your Yemeni / Saudi friend in Birmingham after eating.

Arabic
صَاحِبَك: كَيْف المَطْعَم هَذَا؟ عَجَبَك؟
أَنْت: أَشُوف إِنُّو الأَكْل مَرّة حِلُو، خَاصَّةً الكَبْسَة.
صَاحِبَك: والقَهْوَة العَرَبِيّة؟
أَنْت: صَرَاحَةً ما أَحِبّهَا كَثِير، أَحِبّ الشَّاي أَكْثَر.
صَاحِبَك: أَنَا العَكْس، أَعْشَق القَهْوَة العَرَبِيّة.
Transliteration
ṣāḥibak: kēf il-maṭʿam hādhā? ʿajabak?
anta: ashūf innu al-akl marrah ḥilw, khāṣṣatan il-kabsa.
ṣāḥibak: w-al-gahwah al-ʿarabiyyah?
anta: ṣarāḥatan mā aḥibbha kathīr, aḥibb ash-shāy akthar.
ṣāḥibak: anā il-ʿaks, aʿshag al-gahwah al-ʿarabiyyah.
English
Your friend: How’s this restaurant? Did you like it?
You: I think the food is really nice, especially the kabsa.
Your friend: And the Arabic coffee?
You: Honestly, I don’t like it that much, I like tea more.
Your friend: I’m the opposite, I love Arabic coffee.
Personalise
Change “kabsa / coffee / tea” to your real favourites and rewrite the dialogue.

7. Dialogue 2 – Media & Hobbies

Talking about YouTube, series and football with Arab mates.

Arabic
صَاحِبَك: تُحِبّ تَتْفْلِكْس ولا يوتيوب أَكْثَر؟
أَنْت: أَحِسّ إِنُّو يوتيوب أَفْضَل، أَتْعَلَّم كَثِير مِنُّه.
صَاحِبَك: وتُحِبّ الكُورَة؟
أَنْت: أَحِبّ أَتَفَرَّج، بَسّ ما أَلْعَب كَثِير.
صَاحِبَك: فِي رَأْيِي اللَّعِب أَحْسَن مِن المُشَاهَدَة.
Transliteration
ṣāḥibak: tuḥibb Netflix walla YouTube akthar?
anta: aḥiss innu YouTube afḍal, atʿallam kathīr minnah.
ṣāḥibak: wa tuḥibb al-kōrah?
anta: aḥibb atfarraj, bass mā alʿab kathīr.
ṣāḥibak: fī ra’yī al-laʿib aḥsan min al-mushāhadah.
English
Your friend: Do you like Netflix or YouTube more?
You: I feel YouTube is better, I learn a lot from it.
Your friend: And do you like football?
You: I like watching, but I don’t play much.
Your friend: In my opinion, playing is better than watching.

10. End-of-Lesson Quiz

A. Translate to Arabic
  1. I like Saudi food.
  2. I don’t like very salty food.
  3. Honestly, I think this restaurant is not very good.
  4. I feel Arabic is a bit hard, but nice.
  5. In my opinion, Madīnah is calmer than Makkah.
Sample answers
  • أَحِبّ الأَكْل السُّعُودِي.
  • ما أَحِبّ الأَكْل المَرّة مَالِح.
  • صَرَاحَةً أَشُوف إِنُّو المَطْعَم هَذَا مُو مَرّة زَيْن.
  • أَحِسّ إِنُّو العَرَبِي شُوَيّ صَعْب، بَسّ حِلُو.
  • فِي رَأْيِي المَدِينَة أَهْدَى مِن مَكَّة.
B. Choose the More Natural Dialect Option
  1. “I like tea”
    A) أُحِبُّ الشَّاي
    B) أَحِبّ الشَّاي
  2. “I don’t like this place”
    A) لا أُحِبُّ هذا المَكَان
    B) ما أَحِبّ هَذَا المَكَان
  3. “Honestly, I think it’s nice”
    A) صَرَاحَةً أَشُوف إِنُّو حِلُو
    B) صَرَاحَةً أَرَى أَنَّهُ جَمِيل
Answers
  • 1 → B) أَحِبّ الشَّاي
  • 2 → B) ما أَحِبّ هَذَا المَكَان
  • 3 → A) صَرَاحَةً أَشُوف إِنُّو حِلُو
