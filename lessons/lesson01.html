Lesson 1 – Greetings, Introductions & The Saudi Sound

Start speaking like a real Saudi / Khaleeji – not a textbook robot – with natural greetings, introductions, and your first complete sentences.

Level: A1 → A2 Focus: Speaking & Listening Dialect: Najdi + Hijāzi + Gulf Time: ~90 minutes

1. Overview & Dialect Blend

This lesson gives you the phrases you’ll use with every Arab you ever meet: greetings, “How are you?”, your name, where you’re from, and where you live.

By the end of Lesson 1, you can:
  • Use natural Saudi / Gulf greetings (not stiff fuṣḥā).
  • Introduce yourself: name, origin, where you live.
  • Ask basic questions: “Where are you from?”, “Where do you live?”
  • Use key pronouns (I, you, he, she, we, they).
  • Build simple sentences with the A–B–C formula.
  • Hold your first small conversation in the masjid or shop.
Dialect Blend You’re Learning

You’re learning a functional Saudi Arabic that sounds normal to most Saudis and Khaleejis, and still friendly to other Arabs:

  • 40% Najdi – Riyadh/Qassim base, clear grammar.
  • 30% Hijāzi – Makkah/Jeddah/Madīnah warmth.
  • 20% Gulf – Kuwaiti / Qatari / Emirati flavour.
  • 10% MSA – Islamic phrases & formal bits.

2. Core Greetings (Saudi + Hijāzi + Gulf)

Memorise these. Use them with every Arab you see. This is your daily toolkit.

Universal Islamic Greeting
السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ
as-salāmu ʿalaykum – Peace be upon you.
وَعَلَيْكُم السَّلَام
wa-ʿalaykum as-salām – And peace be upon you.
Saudi / Najdi / Hijāzi Welcomes
هَلّا والله
hallā wallah – “Hey / welcome bro!” (very Saudi/Gulf)
يا هَلا يا مَرْحَبَا
yā halā yā marḥabā – “Welcome, welcome!”
How are you? – Different Flavours
كِيف الحَال؟
kēf il-ḥāl? – How are you? (Hijāzi / general)
وِشّ أَخْبَارَك؟
wish akhbārak? – How are things? (Najdi)
شْلُونَك؟
shlōnak? – How are you? (Gulf)

Choose 1–2 to make “your style”. For example: kēf il-ḥāl? + wish akhbārak?

Quick Speaking Drill
Say each greeting out loud 5 times, with feeling (smile, hand shake etc.).
  • السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ
  • وَعَلَيْكُم السَّلَام
  • هَلّا والله
  • يا هَلا يا مَرْحَبَا
  • كِيف الحَال؟
  • وِشّ أَخْبَارَك؟

3. Introductions: Name, Origin & Where You Live

These are your first proper “I am…” sentences.

My name is…
اِسْمِي زِيشَان
ismī Zīshān – My name is Zeeshan.

Pattern: اسمي + [your name]

  • اسمي محمد – ismī Muḥammad
  • اسمي كريم – ismī Karīm
I am from…
أَنَا مِنْ بِرْمِنْغَهَام
ana min Birmingham – I am from Birmingham.

Pattern: أنا من + place

  • أنا من لندن – ana min London
  • أنا من باكستان – ana min Bākistān
I live in…
أَنَا ساكِن فِي سُولِيهُول
ana sākin fī Solihull – I live in Solihull. (male)

For a female: أنا ساكنة في… – ana sāknah fī…

I am Pakistani / British
أَنَا باكِسْتَانِي
ana bākistānī – I am Pakistani.
أَنَا بْرِيطَانِي
ana brīṭānī – I am British.
Write Your Mini Introduction
In your notebook, write this in Arabic:
  1. My name is ______.
  2. I am from ______.
  3. I live in ______.
  4. I am Pakistani / British.

5. The A–B–C Saudi Sentence Formula

Use this to build sentences without overthinking grammar.

Think of a sentence as:

  • A = WHO? (I / you / he / she…)
  • B = WHAT STATE / ACTION? (tired, happy, want, go…)
  • C = EXTRA INFO (today, now, to the masjid, in Birmingham…)
Examples
أَنَا تَعْبَان اليَوْم
ana taʿbān il-yōm – I’m tired today.
إنت ساكِن فِي سُولِيهُول؟
inta sākin fī Solihull? – Do you live in Solihull?
هِيَّ مَبْسُوطَة كَثِير
hiyya mabsūṭah kathīr – She’s very happy.
أَنَا أَبْغَى قَهْوَة
ana abghā gahwah – I want coffee.
هُوَّ يَرُوح السُّوق
huwwa yrūḥ as-sūg – He goes to the market.
Practice – Build Sentences
Translate using only vocab from this lesson:
  1. I want tea.
  2. She lives in Birmingham.
  3. You (f) are happy today.
  4. They go to London.
Sample answers
  • أنا أبغى شاي
  • هيّ ساكنة في برمنغهام
  • إنتي مبسوطة اليوم
  • همّا يروحوا لندن

6. Dialogue: Meeting at the Masjid

This is your first full Saudi-style conversation.

Arabic
أ: السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ
ب: وَعَلَيْكُم السَّلَام، هَلّا والله!
أ: يا هَلا والله! كِيف الحَال؟
ب: تَمَام، الحَمْدُ لله، وإنت؟
أ: والله مَبْسُوط. اِسْمِي زِيشَان.
ب: تَشَرَّفْنَا يا زِيشَان! أَنَا أَحْمَد.
أ: مِنْ وين؟
ب: أَنَا مِنْ جِدّة، وإنت؟
أ: أَنَا مِنْ بِرْمِنْغَهَام.
ب: وَيْن تِسْكِن؟
أ: أَسْكَن فِي سُولِيهُول.
ب: حَيّاك الله يا أَخُوي!
أ: الله يْحَيّيك!
Transliteration
A: as-salāmu ʿalaykum
B: wa-ʿalaykum as-salām, hallā wallah!
A: yā halā wallah! kēf il-ḥāl?
B: tamām, al-ḥamdulillah, w-inta?
A: wallah mabsūṭ. ismī Zīshān.
B: tasharrafnā yā Zīshān! ana Aḥmad.
A: min wēn?
B: ana min Jiddah, w-inta?
A: ana min Birmingham.
B: wēn tiskin?
A: askin fī Solihull.
B: ḥayyāk Allāh yā akhūy!
A: Allāh yḥayyīk!
English
A: Peace be upon you.
B: And peace be upon you – welcome!
A: Welcome bro! How are you?
B: Fine, al-ḥamdulillah. And you?
A: I’m good, wallah. My name is Zeeshan.
B: Nice to meet you, Zeeshan! I’m Ahmed.
A: From where?
B: I’m from Jeddah. And you?
A: I’m from Birmingham.
B: Where do you live?
A: I live in Solihull.
B: Welcome, my brother!
A: May Allah give life to you too!

7. Masculine & Feminine

Arabic adjectives change slightly for feminine. Saudi keeps it simple.

Common Pattern: -ـة for feminine
Masculine Feminine Meaning
ساكن ساكنة living / residing
تعبان تعبانة tired
مبسوط مبسوطة happy
مشغول مشغولة busy
Convert to Feminine
  1. تعبان
  2. ساكن
  3. مبسوط
Answers
  • تعبانة
  • ساكنة
  • مبسوطة

9. Grammar Practice

A. Complete the Sentences
Use: ساكن – مبسوط – تروح – هوّ
  1. أنا ______ في لندن. (I live in London.)
  2. إنتَ ______ اليوم؟ (Are you happy today?)
  3. هيّ ______ السوق. (She goes to the market.)
  4. ______ زعلان شوي. (He is a bit upset.)
Answers
  1. أنا ساكن في لندن.
  2. إنتَ مبسوط اليوم؟
  3. هيّ تروح السوق.
  4. هوّ زعلان شوي.
B. Masculine → Feminine
  1. هوّ ساكن في الرياض
  2. هوّ مبسوط اليوم
  3. هوّ مشغول شوي
Answers
  • هيّ ساكنة في الرياض
  • هيّ مبسوطة اليوم
  • هيّ مشغولة شوي
