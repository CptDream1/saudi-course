Lesson 8 – Masjid, Dīn & Duʿā

Talk naturally in the masjid: small talk before/after ṣalāh, basic religious vocabulary, and everyday duʿā-style phrases Saudis and Khaleejis actually use.

Level: A1 → A2+ Focus: Masjid & Religion Topic: Dīn & Duʿā Time: ~90 minutes

1. Overview – Sound Like a Practising Muslim in Arabic

You already say “as-salāmu ʿalaykum” and “al-ḥamdulillāh”. Now we build full masjid conversations and duʿā-style replies that Arabs find warm and natural.

By the end of this lesson, you can:
  • Greet brothers in the masjid in a warm Saudi / Hijāzi style.
  • Ask if they prayed, how the khuṭbah was, and how they’re doing.
  • Use common dīn words: ṣalāh, Qurʾān, imām, khuṭbah, wuḍūʾ, janāzah.
  • Use duʿā phrases like “Allāh yjazīk khayr”, “Allāh yshfīk”, “Allāh yuʿīnna”.
  • Have a short chat in the masjid with Saudis, Yemenis and other Arabs in Birmingham.

3. Everyday Duʿā Phrases Arabs Use

These are the “Allāh y…” sentences that make you sound like you grew up around practising Arabs.

Very Common Pious Replies
  • جزاك الله خير – jazāk Allāh khayr – May Allah reward you with good.
  • الله يجزاك خير – Allāh yjzāk khayr – Allah reward you with good. (Najdi/Gulf flavour)
  • الله يكتب أجرك – Allāh yaktib ajrak – May Allah write your reward.
  • الله يوفقك – Allāh ywuffig – May Allah give you success.
  • الله يحفظك – Allāh yaḥfaẓak – May Allah protect you.
  • الله يشفيك – Allāh yshfīk – May Allah cure you.
When Someone Says Good News
  • ما شاء الله – mā shāʼ Allāh – What Allah willed.
  • تبارك الله – tabārak Allāh – Blessed is Allah (often with mā shāʼ Allāh).
  • الله يبارك فيك – Allāh ybārik fīk – May Allah put barakah in it for you.
When Someone is Going / Leaving
  • في أمان الله – fī amān Allāh – In the protection of Allah.
  • الله معك – Allāh maʿak – Allah be with you.
Mini Practice – Which Duʿā?
Which phrase would you use when:
  1. A brother helps you with something heavy.
  2. A friend tells you his wife had a baby.
  3. A friend is ill.
Sample answers
  • جزاك الله خير / الله يجزاك خير
  • ما شاء الله، الله يبارك فيكم
  • الله يشفيك / الله يشفيه

4. Small Talk in the Masjid

These are the lines you’ll actually use in Birmingham masājid with Arabs.

Key Questions
  • صليت؟ – ṣallēt? – Did you pray?
  • حضرت الخُطبة؟ – ḥaḍḍart al-khuṭbah? – Did you attend the khutbah?
  • كيف الخُطبة اليوم؟ – kēf al-khuṭbah il-yōm? – How was the khutbah today?
  • تحب الإمام هنا؟ – tiḥibb al-imām hnā? – Do you like the imam here?
  • في دروس في المسجد؟ – fī drūs fil-masjid? – Are there lessons in the masjid?
Answer Ideas
  • أيوا، صليت مع الإمام – aywa, ṣallēt maʿ al-imām – Yes, I prayed with the imam.
  • الخُطبة كانت حلوة، عن الصبر – al-khuṭbah kānat ḥilwah, ʿan aṣ-ṣabr – The khutbah was nice, about patience.
  • في درس بعد المغرب – fī dars baʿd al-maghrib – There’s a lesson after Maghrib.
  • أحب القرآن بصوت الإمام هنا – aḥibb al-Qurʼān bi-ṣawt al-imām hnā – I love the Qurʾān with the imam’s voice here.
Write & Say – My Masjid
In your notebook, write 3–4 sentences in Arabic about:
  • Which masjid you go to.
  • If you like the imam / the Qurʾān there.
  • If there are darūs or talks.

5. Dialogue 1 – After Jumuʿah in Birmingham

Simple Najdi/Hijāzi-flavoured chat with an Arab brother outside the masjid.

Arabic
أنت: السلام عليكم يا أخي.
هو: وعليكم السلام ورحمة الله، هلا والله.
أنت: كيف الخُطبة اليوم؟
هو: ما شاء الله، كانت جميلة، عن التوبة.
أنت: أيوا، الإمام قرأ قرآن مرّة حلو.
هو: صحيح، الله يحفظه.
أنت: في درس بعد المغرب؟
هو: أي، في درس حديث، تعال إن شاء الله.
أنت: إن شاء الله، جزاك الله خير.
هو: وإيّاك يا حبيب.
Transliteration
anta: as-salāmu ʿalaykum yā akhī.
huwa: wa-ʿalaykum as-salām wa-raḥmatullāh, hallā wallah.
anta: kēf al-khuṭbah il-yōm?
huwa: mā shāʼ Allāh, kānat jamīlah, ʿan at-tawbah.
anta: aywa, al-imām qaraʼ Qurʼān marrah ḥilw.
huwa: ṣaḥīḥ, Allāh yaḥfaẓah.
anta: fī dars baʿd al-maghrib?
huwa: ay, fī dars ḥadīth, taʿāl inshāʼ Allāh.
anta: inshāʼ Allāh, jazāk Allāh khayr.
huwa: wa-iyyāk yā ḥabīb.
English
You: Peace be upon you, my brother.
Him: And peace be upon you and the mercy of Allah – welcome bro.
You: How was the khutbah today?
Him: Mā shā’ Allāh, it was beautiful, about repentance.
You: Yeah, the imam recited Qur’an really nicely.
Him: True, may Allah protect him.
You: Is there a lesson after Maghrib?
Him: Yes, there’s a ḥadīth lesson – come, in shā’ Allah.
You: In shā’ Allah, may Allah reward you with good.
Him: And you too, my dear.
Speaking Drill
Read this out loud 2–3 times. Then change:
  • Topic of khutbah (ṣalāh, ṣabr, Ramaḍān, etc.).
  • Lesson type (Qur’an, fiqh, Arabic).

6. Dialogue 2 – Checking on a Brother Who Was Ill

Use duʿā phrases in a natural short conversation.

Arabic
أنت: السلام عليكم يا شيخ، ما شفناك في المسجد أمس.
هو: وعليكم السلام، والله كنت تعبان شوية.
أنت: الله يشفيك، طهور إن شاء الله.
هو: آمين، الله يجزاك خير.
أنت: تحتاج شيء؟
هو: لا والله، الحمد لله، بس ادعِ لنا.
أنت: إن شاء الله، الله يحفظك.
Transliteration
anta: as-salāmu ʿalaykum yā shaykh, mā shufnāk fil-masjid ams.
huwa: wa-ʿalaykum as-salām, wallah kunt taʿbān shwayyah.
anta: Allāh yshfīk, ṭahūr inshāʼ Allāh.
huwa: āmīn, Allāh yjzāk khayr.
anta: tiḥtāj shay?
huwa: lā wallah, al-ḥamdulillāh, bass idʿī lanā.
anta: inshāʼ Allāh, Allāh yaḥfaẓak.
English
You: Peace be upon you, shaykh – we didn’t see you in the masjid yesterday.
Him: And peace be upon you – honestly I was a bit unwell.
You: May Allah cure you – may it be purification, in shā’ Allah.
Him: Amīn, may Allah reward you with good.
You: Do you need anything?
Him: No, wallah, all praise is for Allah – just make duʿā for us.
You: In shā’ Allah, may Allah protect you.

9. End-of-Lesson Quiz

A. Translate to Arabic
  1. I prayed Jumuʿah in the big masjid.
  2. The khutbah was about patience.
  3. May Allah reward you with good.
  4. Is there a lesson after Maghrib?
B. Choose the More Dialect / Natural Option
  1. “May Allah cure you”
    A) شفاك الله
    B) الله يشفيك
  2. “The khutbah was nice”
    A) الخُطبة كانت جميلة
    B) الخُطبة كانت حلوة
  3. “May Allah put barakah in it for you”
    A) الله يبارك فيك
    B) بارك الله فيكم
