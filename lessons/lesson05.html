Lesson 5 – Food, Drink & Cafés

Order food, ask “What do you want to eat?”, talk about tea, coffee, hunger and favourite dishes – in natural Saudi / Gulf Arabic, not textbook fuṣḥā.

Level: A1 → A2+ Focus: Speaking & Listening Topic: Food, Drink & Cafés Time: ~90 minutes

1. Overview – Food Talk = Instant Connection

Arabs love food talk. If you can chat about food & drink, you can bond quickly with Saudis, Yemenis, Egyptians, Syrians, etc. – at home, in cafés, and at gatherings.

By the end of this lesson, you can:
  • Say you’re hungry/thirsty and what you want to eat or drink.
  • Order basic food and drinks in a café or small restaurant.
  • Ask someone what they want and talk about favourite foods.
  • Use polite phrases like “please”, “that’s enough”, “thank you”.
  • Handle a simple “What do you want?” conversation fully in dialect.

3. Key Café & Food Phrases

These are phrases you’ll use constantly in restaurants, at home, and at Arab friends’ houses.

I want / Do you want…?
  • أبغى موية – abghā moyyah – I want water.
  • أبغى شاي بحليب – abghā shāy bi-ḥalīb – I want tea with milk.
  • تبغى قهوة؟ – tibghā gahwah? – Do you (m) want coffee?
  • تبغي عصير؟ – tibghīn ʿaṣīr? – Do you (f) want juice?
Ordering in a Simple Way
  • لو سمحت، أبغى شاي نعناع – law samaḥt, abghā shāy naʿnāʿ – Excuse me / please, I’d like mint tea.
  • لو سمحت، واحد موية صغيرة – law samaḥt, wāḥid moyyah ṣaghīrah – One small water, please.
  • أبغى ساندويتش دجاج – abghā sāndawitch dijāj – I want a chicken sandwich.
Politeness & Reactions
  • شكراً – shukran – Thanks.
  • يعطيك العافية – yaʿṭīk al-ʿāfiyah – May Allah give you wellness (thank you for your effort).
  • كفاية، شكراً – kafāyah, shukran – That’s enough, thanks.
  • الأكل مرّة لذيذ – al-akl marrah ladhīdh – The food is really tasty.

4. Food Verbs – Want, Eat, Drink, Like

You already know some of these – now use them properly with food.

Arabic Translit Meaning
أبغى abghā I want
آكل ākul I eat
أشرب ashrab I drink
أحب uḥibb / aḥibb I like / I love
Examples
أنا جوعان، أبغى آكل كبسة.
ana jawʿān, abghā ākul kabsa – I’m hungry, I want to eat kabsa.
أحب القهوة العربية.
aḥibb al-gahwah al-ʿarabiyyah – I love Arabic coffee.
أشرب شاي بعد الأكل.
ashrab shāy baʿd al-akl – I drink tea after food.
Translate – Verb + Food
Using only verbs from the table and food words from this lesson, translate:
  1. I want water.
  2. I eat rice and chicken.
  3. I like Saudi food.
  4. I drink coffee in the morning.
Sample answers
  • أبغى موية.
  • آكل رز ودجاج.
  • أحب الأكل السعودي.
  • أشرب قهوة الصباح.

5. Dialogue – At the Café in Birmingham

A simple but realistic Saudi / Gulf style conversation in a café with an Arab waiter.

Arabic
الجرسون: السلام عليكم.
أنت: وعليكم السلام، لو سمحت…
الجرسون: تفضل، إيش تبغى تشرب؟
أنت: أبغى شاي بحليب، وموية صغيرة.
الجرسون: تمام، تبغى أكل بعدين؟
أنت: إيه، بعدين أبغى ساندويتش دجاج.
الجرسون: حاضر، شيء ثاني؟
أنت: لا، كفاية، شكراً. يعطيك العافية.
الجرسون: الله يعافيك.
Transliteration
al-jarsūn: as-salāmu ʿalaykum.
anta: wa-ʿalaykum as-salām, law samaḥt…
al-jarsūn: tfaḍḍal, ēsh tibghā tishrab?
anta: abghā shāy bi-ḥalīb, wa-moyyah ṣaghīrah.
al-jarsūn: tamām, tibghā akl baʿdēn?
anta: ēh, baʿdēn abghā sāndawitch dijāj.
al-jarsūn: ḥāḍir, shay thānī?
anta: lā, kafāyah, shukran. yaʿṭīk al-ʿāfiyah.
al-jarsūn: Allāh yaʿāfīk.
English
Waiter: Peace be upon you.
You: And peace be upon you. Excuse me…
Waiter: Go ahead – what would you like to drink?
You: I’d like tea with milk, and a small water.
Waiter: Okay – do you want food later?
You: Yeah, later I want a chicken sandwich.
Waiter: Sure, anything else?
You: No, that’s enough, thanks. May Allah give you wellness.
Waiter: May Allah grant you wellness too.
Speaking Drill
Read the whole dialogue out loud 3 times. Then change:
  • Drink: make it قهوة عربية or عصير.
  • Food: change to كبسة or مندي (imagine a restaurant).

6. Small Talk – “What Do You Like to Eat?”

Use this with Arab friends at gatherings, after dars, or at someone’s house.

Key Questions
  • إيش تحب تاكل؟ – ēsh tiḥibb tākul? – What do you like to eat? (to a male)
  • إيش تحبي تاكلي؟ – ēsh tiḥibbī tākulī? – What do you like to eat? (to a female)
  • تحب الأكل السعودي؟ – tiḥibb al-akl as-saʿūdī? – Do you like Saudi food?
Answer Ideas
  • أحب الكبسة، خصوصاً مع دجاج – aḥibb al-kabsa, khuṣūṣan maʿ dijāj – I like kabsa, especially with chicken.
  • أحب الأكل اليمني، مرّة لذيذ – aḥibb al-akl al-yamanī, marrah ladhīdh – I love Yemeni food, it’s really tasty.
  • ما أحب الأكل الثقيل بالليل – mā aḥibb al-akl ath-thagīl bil-lēl – I don’t like heavy food at night.
Your Turn – Write & Say
In your notebook, write 3–4 sentences about:
  • What food you like.
  • What food you don’t like.
  • What you usually drink with food.
