Lesson 9 – Health & Feeling Unwell

Tell Arabs you’re tired, sick, have a headache, need a doctor, or just “finished” – and respond with natural duʿā phrases when others are ill.

Level: A1 → A2+ Focus: Speaking Topic: Health & Illness Time: ~90 minutes

1. Overview – “Bro I’m Finished” in Saudi Arabic

Health talk is classic small talk with uncles and friends. You’ll use this when you’re actually sick, when you’re just tired, and when checking on others.

By the end of this lesson, you can:
  • Say you’re tired, sick, or in pain, and where it hurts.
  • Say basic problems: headache, stomach, flu, sore throat.
  • Ask someone how they’re feeling and respond with duʿā.
  • Say “I need a doctor / pharmacy” in simple dialect.
  • Describe basic health stuff in Umrah / Hajj situations.

2. Core Vocabulary – Feelings & Symptoms

Some words you know already; now you’ll use them in health context.

2.1 How You Feel

3. Key Phrases – Saying You’re Unwell

These are the exact lines you’ll say in Birmingham to your Yemeni / Egyptian / Saudi mates.

Basic “I’m not okay” Sentences
  • اليوم أنا تعبان شوي – il-yōm ana taʿbān shway – Today I’m a bit tired.
  • اليوم ما أنا طيب – il-yōm mā ana ṭayyib – Today I’m not well.
  • عندي زكام – ʿindī zukām – I have a cold / flu.
  • عندي حرارة – ʿindī ḥarārah – I have a fever.
Where It Hurts
  • راسي يوجعني – rāsī yawgʿanī – My head hurts.
  • بطني يوجعني – baṭnī yawgʿanī – My stomach hurts.
  • حلقي يوجعني – ḥalgī yawgʿanī – My throat hurts.
  • ظهري يوجعني – ẓahrī yawgʿanī – My back hurts.
Needing Help
  • أحتاج دكتور – aḥtāj duktūr – I need a doctor.
  • في صيدلية قريبة؟ – fī ṣaydaliyyah qarībah? – Is there a nearby pharmacy?
  • أقدر آخذ إجازة اليوم؟ – agdar ākhudh ijāzah il-yōm? – Can I take a day off today?
Mini Practice – Your Situation
Say in Arabic (out loud, then write in your notebook):
  1. Today I am very tired.
  2. I have a headache.
  3. I need a doctor.
Sample answers
  • اليوم أنا تعبان مرّة – il-yōm ana taʿbān marrah
  • راسي يوجعني – rāsī yawgʿanī
  • أحتاج دكتور – aḥtāj duktūr

4. Asking About Health & Responding

Mix Lesson 8 duʿā with health vocab to sound caring and close.

Checking on Someone
  • كيف صحتك اليوم؟ – kēf ṣiḥt-ak il-yōm? – How is your health today?
  • أحسن شوية؟ – aḥsan shwayyah? – Any better?
  • لسه تعبان؟ – lissa taʿbān? – Still tired / unwell?
Replies
  • الحمد لله، أحسن – al-ḥamdulillāh, aḥsan – Alḥamdulillāh, better.
  • لسه تعبان شوية – lissa taʿbān shwayyah – Still a bit tired.
  • اليوم أحسن من أمس – il-yōm aḥsan min ams – Today is better than yesterday.
Duʿā Responses
  • الله يشفيك – Allāh yshfīk – May Allah cure you.
  • طهور إن شاء الله – ṭahūr inshāʼ Allāh – A purification, in shā’ Allah.
  • لا تنسَ تدعي لنا بعد – lā tinsa tidʿī lanā baʿd – Don’t forget to make duʿā for us as well.

5. Dialogue 1 – Telling a Friend You’re Ill (WhatsApp / In Person)

Short, realistic Najdi/Hijāzi-flavoured chat you could literally send on WhatsApp.

Arabic
صاحبك: يا زيشان، وينك أمس؟ ما شفناك في المسجد.
أنت: والله كنت تعبان شوي، عندي زكام.
صاحبك: الله يشفيك يا أخي.
أنت: آمين، جزاك الله خير.
صاحبك: الحين أحسن؟
أنت: الحمد لله، اليوم أحسن من أمس.
Transliteration
ṣāḥbak: yā Zīshān, wēnak ams? mā shufnāk fil-masjid.
anta: wallah kunt taʿbān shway, ʿindī zukām.
ṣāḥbak: Allāh yshfīk yā akhī.
anta: āmīn, jazāk Allāh khayr.
ṣāḥbak: al-ḥīn aḥsan?
anta: al-ḥamdulillāh, il-yōm aḥsan min ams.
English
Your friend: Zeeshan, where were you yesterday? We didn’t see you in the masjid.
You: Honestly I was a bit tired, I have a cold.
Your friend: May Allah cure you, my brother.
You: Amīn, may Allah reward you with good.
Your friend: Are you better now?
You: Alḥamdulillāh, today is better than yesterday.
Speaking Drill
Read out loud 2–3 times. Then change:
  • “cold” → “fever” (حرارة).
  • “yesterday” → “two days ago” (قبل يومين).

6. Dialogue 2 – Simple Doctor Visit

Not deep medical Arabic – just enough to explain basic problems.

Arabic
الدكتور: السلام عليكم، تفضل، ايش فيك؟
أنت: وعليكم السلام، دكتور… عندي وجع في راسي وبطني.
الدكتور: من متى؟
أنت: من أمس بالليل.
الدكتور: عندك حرارة؟
أنت: أي، شوية.
الدكتور: طيب، لا تخاف، إن شاء الله بسيط.
أنت: إن شاء الله، شكراً دكتور.
Transliteration
ad-duktūr: as-salāmu ʿalaykum, tfaḍḍal, ēsh fīk?
anta: wa-ʿalaykum as-salām, duktūr… ʿindī wajaʿ fī rāsī wa-baṭnī.
ad-duktūr: min mitā?
anta: min ams bil-lēl.
ad-duktūr: ʿindak ḥarārah?
anta: ay, shwayyah.
ad-duktūr: ṭayyib, lā tkhāf, inshāʼ Allāh basīṭ.
anta: inshāʼ Allāh, shukran duktūr.
English
Doctor: Peace be upon you, come in – what’s wrong with you?
You: And peace be upon you – doctor, I have pain in my head and stomach.
Doctor: Since when?
You: Since last night.
Doctor: Do you have a temperature?
You: Yes, a bit.
Doctor: Okay, don’t worry – in shā’ Allah it’s something simple.
You: In shā’ Allah, thank you doctor.

8. Speaking Drills – My Health Today

Combine health + time + duʿā so it sounds like you grew up with Arab uncles.

أمس كنت مريض، اليوم الحمد لله أحسن – ams kunt marīḍ, il-yōm al-ḥamdulillāh aḥsan
راسي يوجعني، بس لازم أروح الشغل – rāsī yawgʿanī, bass lāzim arūḥ ash-shughol
الله يشفي كل مريض – Allāh yshfī kull marīḍ – May Allah cure every sick person.

9. End-of-Lesson Quiz

A. Translate to Arabic
  1. Today I am very tired.
  2. My stomach hurts.
  3. I have a fever and a cold.
  4. May Allah cure you.
B. Choose the More Dialect / Natural Option
  1. “I have a cold”
    A) عندي زكام
    B) أنا مصاب بالزكام
  2. “My head hurts”
    A) عندي ألم في الرأس
    B) راسي يوجعني
  3. “I need a doctor”
    A) أحتاج دكتور
    B) يجب أن أذهب إلى الطبيب
