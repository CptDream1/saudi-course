Lesson 10 – Work, Study & Future Plans

Talk about your job, madrasa, what you’re studying, and what you want to do in the future – the classic “So, what do you do, akhī?” conversation with Arab friends.

Level: A2-ish Focus: Speaking Topic: Work & Study Time: ~90 minutes

1. Overview – “What Do You Do?” in Saudi Arabic

Arabs love asking what you do, what you study, and what you want to be. After this lesson, you can answer that comfortably in your Najdi / Hijāzi / Gulf mix.

By the end of this lesson, you can:
  • Say if you work, study, both, or neither right now.
  • Describe your job (teacher, engineer, shop, driver, etc.).
  • Talk about what you study (college, course, madrasa, ḥifẓ).
  • Express simple future plans and dreams (“I want to be…”).
  • Handle small talk about work/study with Saudis, Yemenis, Masris & co.

4. Key Phrases – “What Do You Do / Study?”

These questions come up in almost every new friendship.

Questions
  • إيش تشتغل؟ – ēsh tishtaghal? – What do you work as? (Najdi/Hijāzi)
  • فين تشتغل؟ – fēn tishtaghal? – Where do you work? (Hijāzi)
  • تدرس ولا تشتغل؟ – tadrus walla tishtaghal? – Do you study or work?
  • إيش تدرس؟ – ēsh tadrus? – What do you study?
  • إيش تخصصك؟ – ēsh takhaṣṣuṣak? – What’s your specialisation?
Answer Patterns
  • أشتغل مدرس في مدرسة إسلامية – ashtaghal mudarris fī madrasa islāmiyyah – I work as a teacher in an Islamic school.
  • أدرس هندسة – adrus handasah – I study engineering.
  • أدرس في الكلية، تخصصي طيران – adrus fil-kulliyyah, takhaṣṣuṣī ṭayarān – I study in college, my major is aviation.
  • ما أشتغل الحين، أدرس بس – mā ashtaghal al-ḥīn, adrus bass – I don’t work now, I just study.

5. Future Plans – “I Want to Be…”

This links to future tense later, but for now we use “abghā” + present.

Useful Patterns
  • أبغى أكون مهندس – abghā akūn muhandis – I want to be an engineer.
  • أبغى أكون طيار – abghā akūn ṭayyār – I want to be a pilot.
  • أبغى أكمّل دراسة في الجامعة – abghā akammil dirāsa fil-jāmiʿah – I want to continue studying at university.
  • إن شاء الله في المستقبل أبغى أشتغل في السعودية – inshāʼ Allāh fil-mustaqbal abghā ashtaghal fis-Saʿūdiyyah – In shā’ Allah in the future I want to work in Saudi.
Write Your Future
In your notebook, write 3 sentences starting with:
  • إن شاء الله في المستقبل… – inshā’ Allāh fil-mustaqbal…

6. Dialogue 1 – Work/Study Chat After Masjid

This is literally the type of convo you’ll have in Birmingham with a Saudi / Khaleeji brother.

Arabic
هو: السلام عليكم يا أخي، أول مرة أشوفك هنا.
أنت: وعليكم السلام، أيوا، أنا جديد شوية في المسجد.
هو: ما شاء الله، إيش تشتغل؟
أنت: أشتغل مدرس في مدرسة إسلامية، وأدرس كمان.
هو: ما شاء الله، إيش تدرس؟
أنت: أدرس هندسة طيران في الكلية.
هو: جميل، إن شاء الله تصير مهندس قوي.
أنت: آمين، الله يجزاك خير يا أخي.
Transliteration
huwa: as-salāmu ʿalaykum yā akhī, awwal marrah ashūfak hnā.
anta: wa-ʿalaykum as-salām, aywa, ana jadīd shwayyah fil-masjid.
huwa: mā shāʼ Allāh, ēsh tishtaghal?
anta: ashtaghal mudarris fī madrasa islāmiyyah, wa adrus kamān.
huwa: mā shāʼ Allāh, ēsh tadrus?
anta: adrus handasat ṭayarān fil-kulliyyah.
huwa: jamīl, inshāʼ Allāh tiṣīr muhandis gawi.
anta: āmīn, Allāh yjzāk khayr yā akhī.
English
Him: Peace be upon you, my brother – first time I see you here.
You: And peace be upon you – yeah, I’m a bit new in this masjid.
Him: Mā shā’ Allah – what do you do (for work)?
You: I work as a teacher in an Islamic school, and I study as well.
Him: Mā shā’ Allah – what do you study?
You: I study aeronautical engineering in college.
Him: Nice – in shā’ Allah you’ll become a strong engineer.
You: Amīn, may Allah reward you with good, my brother.
Personalise It
Change the dialogue to your real:
  • Job type (teacher / shop / driver / etc.).
  • Course (engineering / IT / Islamic studies / etc.).

7. Dialogue 2 – Talking About Future Plans

Mix “abghā” + future vibes with some dīn flavour.

Arabic
صاحبك: طيب، بعد ما تخلص دراسة، إيش ناوي تسوي؟
أنت: إن شاء الله أبغى أشتغل في مجال الطيران.
صاحبك: في بريطانيا ولا برّة؟
أنت: يمكن هنا، ويمكن لو في فرصة حلوة في الخليج.
صاحبك: الله يوفقك يا رجل.
أنت: آمين، وإنت؟ إيش خطتك؟
صاحبك: أبغى أكمل قرآن وأدرس شوية شريعة.
Transliteration
ṣāḥbak: ṭayyib, baʿd mā tkhalliṣ dirāsah, ēsh nāwī tsawwī?
anta: inshāʼ Allāh abghā ashtaghal fī majāl aṭ-ṭayarān.
ṣāḥbak: fī Britānyā walla barra?
anta: yumkin hnā, wa yumkin law fī furṣah ḥilwah fil-khalīj.
ṣāḥbak: Allāh ywuffigak yā rajul.
anta: āmīn, w-inta? ēsh khaṭṭak?
ṣāḥbak: abghā akammil Qurʼān wa adrus shwayyah sharīʿah.
English
Your friend: Okay, after you finish studying, what are you planning to do?
You: In shā’ Allah I want to work in the aviation field.
Your friend: In Britain or abroad?
You: Maybe here, and maybe if there’s a nice opportunity in the Gulf.
Your friend: May Allah give you success, man.
You: Amīn – and you? What’s your plan?
Your friend: I want to complete (memorising) Qur’an and study some sharīʿah.

10. End-of-Lesson Quiz

A. Translate to Arabic
  1. What do you work as?
  2. I work as a teacher and I study as well.
  3. What do you study?
  4. In the future I want to work in Saudi.
B. Choose the More Dialect / Natural Option
  1. “What do you work as?”
    A) ما هي وظيفتك؟
    B) إيش تشتغل؟
  2. “I study at university”
    A) أدرس في الجامعة
    B) أنا في الجامعة للدراسة
  3. “I want to be an engineer”
    A) أبغى أكون مهندس
    B) أريد أن أصبح مهندساً
