Saudi Arabic Course – Home

Your 6-month path to speaking Najdi/Hijāzi Saudi Arabic with a Gulf twist – enough to chat with Saudis, Yemenis, Syrians, Palestinians, Egyptians & more in Birmingham, the UK, and during ʿUmrah & Ḥajj.

Target level: A2 → early B1 Focus: Speaking & Listening Length: 36 lessons (~6 months)

How to Use This Course

This site is your personal workbook + textbook. Every lesson is its own page with vocab, grammar, dialogues, exercises, and an answer key.

  1. Start with Lesson 1. Get comfy with greetings, basic sentences, and the feel of the dialect.
  2. Do 2–3 lessons per week. Each one should take you around 60–90 minutes if you actually do the exercises.
  3. Speak out loud. Use the dialogues and speaking drills. Don’t just read silently.
  4. Use the answer sections honestly. Mark your work, see what you’re missing, then repeat.

Course Roadmap (Quick Overview)

Here’s what the 6 months roughly look like.

Phase 1 – Survival & Foundations (Lessons 1–10)

  • Greetings, introductions, family, daily routine, food & drink.
  • Basic sentence building, present tense, “I want / I go / I live…”.
  • Masjid Arabic, simple shopping, basic directions.

Phase 2 – Real-Life Conversations (Lessons 11–24)

  • Past and future tenses, telling short stories, complaining, joking.
  • Phone/WhatsApp talk, work & study, health, travel, problems.
  • Deeper convo with Arabs in Birmingham about life, family, dīn.

Phase 3 – Fluency Push (Lessons 25–36)

  • Media Arabic (songs, series), slang, Najdi/Hijāzi differences.
  • High-pressure situations (airport, police, border, crowded Ḥaram).
  • Final “project”: holding a 10–15 minute conversation in pure dialect.

Where to Start

You don’t need to overthink it. Just start and let the reps build up.

  • Click “Lesson 1 – Greetings & Introductions” in the sidebar.
  • Read through slowly, repeat the Arabic out loud.
  • Do the exercises, then check your answers.
  • Use anything you learn with real Arabs the same week.
